Description of the Spring Hill Boardwalk Problem as of May 2022

The pictures in the following were taken May 3 and May 5 2022, after a 2 week dry spell that had dried out most trails.

At the East end of the long boardwalk on the Spring Hill main trail there is a 30ft long depressed trail area without drainage that collects rainwater and keeps it for weeks.

Two walk-arounds have been made on the North side (right side in picture), an inner (with all the mud) made when the trail had rainwater, and on outer (further right) when the trail and inner walk-around was flooded by the swamp West stream in Fall 2021. Both walk-arounds join near the board-walk (mud near boardwalk end).

Topography does not seem to permit further development of walk-arounds.

The swamp whose several streams flow under the long boardwalk has been filling up and moving West. Currently most water on the West side of the swamp flows through the West stream (video) which sometimes overflows to an overflow area just above the trail, and from that the trail floods.

Water on the West side of the swamp used to flow through the East branch of the West stream, but that has been substantially blocked by multiple fallen tree trunks and other matter.

Options are:

(A) Extend the boardwalk to cover the entire depression. There appear to be some high stones that would have to be avoided or gone over. Length should be about four 8-ft sections. Measurements need to be made and sill locations need to be found before proceeding further.

(B) Clear the East extension of the West stream, so the East extension carries most of the water now carried by the West stream. Clear the West stream and mitigate any mud on the walk-arounds.

(C) Leave things be until we are forced to do (A) or (B), which might never happen.
